Max S. Dunn
President and CEO
TeamStream, Inc.

Max Dunn, president and CEO, is the founder of TeamStream. A seasoned entrepreneur and technologist, Max has been a major contributor to the technical direction of TeamDrive as well as overseeing the business operations of the company. With more than 18 years of experience in programming and hardware design - as well as 14 years running a company - Max possesses a unique skill set that is well suited to technology startups.

His technical knowledge encompasses Internet and web technology as well as software developed for IBM, Microsoft, Bertelsmann, Cincinnati Bell, Guidant and Intuit. For over six years Max also managed international development teams, in locations as remote as Russia, India and the Czech Republic.

Max's early career spanned positions at Lawrence Berkeley Laboratory, Hughes Aircraft and Ford Aerospace where he worked on projects ranging from missile simulation to research into trends associated with global warming. As a consultant for Marosi Company, Max worked with modifications to computer numeric control equipment. Recruited by InsMark to be their Director of Operations, Max managed a team of nine people involved in writing software, developing documentation, testing software, providing technical and product support and manufacturing products.

In 1988 Max founded Micro System Designs, Inc. (MSD) to address a then-unmet need: to develop, market and sell floppy disk duplication software and hardware that would take advantage of advances in auto-loading equipment. Over the course of eleven years, the company became a worldwide standard for PC-based floppy disk duplication. MSD was sold in 1999.

Concurrently, Max started a second company, MaxLock, in 1997. That company focused on developing computer and Internet security products. Its popular WebPasskey program, which automatically filled in password and contact information on web pages, was sold in 2000 and the rest of its assets, including its high-performance IP core products, were sold in 2001.

In February 2000, Max started TeamStream to connect virtual teams online. TeamDrive, the company's flagship product, turns the Internet into a securely shared hard drive. The first version was released in June 2000.

Max graduated with honors from the University of California, Santa Cruz with a Bachelor's degree in Computer and Information Science.

Jim Brush
VP Sales and Marketing

Jim Brush is responsible for worldwide sales, channel development, marketing and communications . A seasoned sales executive, marketer and trainer, he is a veteran of twenty-six years of sales and marketing within high technology firms. Jim’s career spans sales and sales management responsibilities for Peregrine Systems, Sterling Software, Quest Software, Boole and Babbage, Compuware, Qualtrack, and the Xerox Corporation.

For Sterling Software, Jim planned the sales and marketing strategies leading to the launch of a series of software compression products nationwide - and the company’s most successful new product introduction. For Peregrine and Xerox Corporation, Jim identified market opportunities, sized the markets, developed the messages for those markets and directed the sales activities into those segments.

Throughout his career Jim has worked with innovative technologies, helping to position and price products for successful market penetration. He enjoys hiring, training and growing sales organizations and positioning and promoting products.

Jaroslav Dostal
Vice President of Development
TeamStream, Inc.

Jaroslav Dostal is the Managing Director of TeamStream's Euclio Division and its Vice President of Research and Development. As Managing Director Jarek is responsible for the recruitment, hiring, training and administration of the Euclio Division. As Vice President of Research and Development, he is responsible for the day-to-day direction of the off-shore application development team. Jaroslav's substantial experience and industry expertise enable him to successfully manage large projects.

Jaroslav's early interest in technology led to a degree in mechanical engineering at the Technical University in Brno, Czech Republic. He began his career as a CAD systems programmer and design engineer, developing computer applications, but later took advantage of his language skills-he speaks Czech, English and German fluently-to pursue independent contract work in both Austria and Germany. Those assignments included translation of software documentation, software development, programming Windows and OS/2 applications and database programming.

When Jaroslav returned full-time to the Czech Republic in 1995, he took a position at Spin s.r.o. ultimately becoming the Software Development manager, responsible for municipal information systems. In 1996 Jaroslav joined the Austrian branch of Bull International, IMEX VANICEK where, as the Project Manager, Jaroslav was responsible for systems and projects built on Windows 3.1/ 95/ NT as well as database management projects.

In 1999, he was recruited to Euclio to grow and manage a team focused on developing both software and hardware for TeamDrive. Jaroslav's technical knowledge includes application development in C++, Visual Basic, Microsoft SQL server, Windows and OS/2, Microsoft C, FORTRAN and Pascal as well as database programming.
