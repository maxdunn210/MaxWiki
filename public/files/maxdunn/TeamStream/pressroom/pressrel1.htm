FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASE

SAN JOSE, California, June 19 /PRNewswire/ -- TeamStream, an Internet collaboration infrastructure software start-up, oversubscribed its recent seed funding round for its first product TeamDrive. TeamDrive is the first product to allow virtual team members using any Windows program to collaborate and share documents.

“The venture capital market recognizes the incredible market potential of TeamDrive,” said Rick C. Garian, President of CEO of TeamStream. “This is the first product of its kind, allowing virtual team members to collaborate securely on any document while preserving its integrity. TeamDrive users have access to the same security and collaboration tools as users working on a LAN.”

TeamStream’s Internet collaboration infrastructure TeamDrive provides a suite of solutions that allows virtual teams to work together quickly, easily and securely over the Internet. The technology is applicable for Online Storage Services (OSS), Virtual Private Networks (VPNs), Application Service Providers (ASPs), Enterprise Information Portals (EIPs) and Internet Service Providers (ISPs). With TeamStream's products, people no longer need to transfer their work to a CD or floppy disk.

The seed round funding will allow TeamStream to complete development of TeamDrive and raise its 1st round of funding. More information on TeamDrive can be found at http://www.teamstream.com/products.htm.

About TeamStream

TeamStream, Inc. develops Internet collaboration infrastructure products enabling virtual teams to work together smoothly and securely. Whether its customers are business or home PC users, TeamStream has the right tools available that easily collaborate effectively via the Internet.

Formed in January 2000, TeamStream is based in SanJose, California.
