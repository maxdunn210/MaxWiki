Business 

TeamStream is in the business of connecting virtual teams online. TeamStream's software products enhance the ability of mobile users and virtual teams to access files from any geographic location and to collaborate and share information via the Internet. The company's flagship product, TeamDrive, is built on proprietary technology that maps a drive-letter to an Internet server, effectively turning the Internet into a secure, shared virtual hard drive. With the dramatic increase in the numbers of people working collaboratively and also working remotely or while traveling, TeamDrive addresses the needs of today's computing environment.

Target Market

More than 90% of all companies are using some number of virtual teams to conduct company business. In addition, there has been a worldwide explosion of telecommuters (Gartner Group projects 137 million telecommuters by 2003) also needing access to the resources within their office environments. TeamStream targets the collaboration needs of these virtual teams, telecommuters and other mobile professionals. The product ensures that team members can update, share and modify files and documents both in real time and offline, regardless of time zones or geographic location.

TeamStream offers customized, OEM versions of its software to Online Storage Services (OSSes), Web-based file management providers, document management providers and corporations. These OEM versions are pre-configured, private labeled and customized to ensure that the product works easily and effectively in the customers/partners' computing environment.

The Products

TeamDrive, the company's flagship product, has been developed to provide access to files and documents easily and securely for members of virtual teams. The innovative TeamDrive software turns the Internet into a securely shared, virtual hard drive, enabling users to collaborate and share information whether at work or while away.

The TeamStream product line consists of separate client and server software. The TeamDrive Client runs on the clients' local computers while the TeamDrive Server software runs on an Internet server.

TeamDrive features drive-letter mapping, local caching, 'instant saves', and compression enables any standard Windows file to be easily opened and edited. Users feel as if they are interacting with their local drive while, in fact, TeamDrive is storing their data in a secure Internet location.

To protect data, TeamDrive ensures that the data is encrypted as it travels over the Internet via secure SSL encrypted connections, the most sophisticated encryption technology currently available. When there is either no Internet connection available or when users are traveling, TeamDrive's offline feature allows users to continue working effectively offline and ensures that the next time these users connect to the Internet, all changes are automatically synchronized to the server.

The TeamLinks feature offers a more desirable alternative to sending email attachments multiple times. With TeamLinks, recipients view and edit the original documents themselves, not copies of the documents. This eliminates versioning problems by providing the latest version of the document for viewing and editing. TeamLinks also provides "whoops" control, allowing the sender to update TeamLinked-attached documents, even after they are sent.

In addition to working with the TeamDrive Server, the TeamDrive Client can be customized to work with most WebDAV, FTP, HTTP, XML, SOAP, CGI, ASP and proprietary servers. The TeamDrive Client works on all Windows platforms.

The TeamDrive Server offers advanced features including encrypted and compressed transfers, sharing, locking and remote management; it works with all Windows platforms and Linux.
